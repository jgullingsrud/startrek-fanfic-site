# Seven of Nine

**Seven of Nine, Tertiary Adjunct of Unimatrix 01** (born Annika Hansen) is a former Borg drone who was liberated by the crew of USS Voyager and became one of Starfleet's most unique officers. Her journey from hive mind to individual, from machine to human, represents one of the most compelling character arcs in Star Trek history.

## Background

- **Birth Name**: Annika Hansen
- **Borg Designation**: Seven of Nine, Tertiary Adjunct of Unimatrix 01
- **Born**: 2350 (approximately)
- **Assimilated**: 2356 (age 6)
- **Liberated**: 2374
- **Species**: Human with extensive Borg modifications
- **Position**: Astrometrics Officer, USS Voyager

## Early Life

### The Hansen Family

**Parents**:
- Magnus and Erin Hansen, exobiologists
- Studied the Borg independently
- Obsessed with learning about the Collective
- Took their young daughter on their research vessel

**The Raven**:
- Family ship used to study Borg
- Modified to avoid Borg detection
- Followed Borg cube into Delta Quadrant
- Pushed their luck too far

### Assimilation (2356)

**Loss of Humanity**:
- Borg adapted to their ship's defenses
- Boarded the Raven
- Assimilated entire family
- Six-year-old Annika became Seven of Nine
- Lost childhood, family, individuality
- Spent 18 years as Borg drone

## Life as a Borg

### The Collective

For 18 years, Seven existed as part of the hive:
- No individual thought
- Part of trillions of minds
- Participated in countless assimilations
- Accessed vast Borg knowledge
- Experienced the "perfection" of the Collective

**High-Ranking Drone**:
- Tertiary Adjunct of Unimatrix 01
- Close to Borg Queen
- Trusted with important tasks
- Representative of the Collective

## Liberation and Voyager

### Severed from the Collective (2374)

**Alliance with Voyager**:
- Borg allied with Voyager against Species 8472
- Seven assigned to work with Voyager crew
- Janeway severed her from Collective
- Forced return to humanity

**Initial Resistance**:
- Wanted to return to the Borg
- Viewed individuality as chaos
- Considered humanity inferior
- Tried to contact the Collective
- Struggled with independence

**Physical Transformation**:
- Doctor removed most cybernetic implants
- Retained some necessary implants
- Regained human appearance (mostly)
- Visible Borg technology remained
- Wore modified Borg bodysuit

### Becoming Human

**Reclaiming Identity**:
- Initially insisted on Borg designation
- Refused name "Annika"
- Eventually accepted "Seven of Nine"
- Later embraced "Seven"
- Gradual journey to individuality

**Social Development**:
- Relearned human interaction
- Studied human behavior
- Made social mistakes
- Developed friendships slowly
- Struggled with emotions

## Final Assessment

Seven of Nine's journey from Borg drone to individual represents one of Star Trek's most powerful redemption stories. She proved that humanity isn't something you're born withâ€”it's something you choose.

Her struggle to reclaim her individuality, to understand emotions while retaining logic, and to find her place between two worlds made her one of the most complex characters in Star Trek. She showed that perfection isn't found in uniformity but in embracing our unique, imperfect selves.

Seven demonstrated that it's never too late to become who you're meant to be, that recovery from trauma is possible, and that the courage to be vulnerable is the ultimate strength. She was Borg. She became human. She became herself.
