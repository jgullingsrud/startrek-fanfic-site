# Jean-Luc Picard

**Captain Jean-Luc Picard** is one of Starfleet's most celebrated and distinguished officers, best known for commanding the USS Enterprise-D and USS Enterprise-E. A thoughtful diplomat, skilled tactician, and passionate advocate for peace and exploration, Picard has left an indelible mark on the Federation and beyond.

## Early Life

Born on July 13, 2305, in La Barre, France, on Earth, Jean-Luc Picard grew up in his family's vineyard. Despite his family's deep connection to the land and winemaking tradition, young Picard dreamed of the stars.

## Starfleet Career

### Early Years

Picard entered Starfleet Academy in 2323. During his academy years, he became the only freshman to win the Academy Marathon. He graduated in 2327 and was assigned to the USS Reliant.

### USS Stargazer

Picard's first command came when he assumed control of the USS Stargazer after the captain was killed in an attack. He commanded the Stargazer for over 20 years until it was destroyed in battle with an unknown vessel using what would later be known as the "Picard Maneuver."

### USS Enterprise-D

In 2364, Picard was given command of the Galaxy-class USS Enterprise-D. Under his leadership, the Enterprise:

- Made first contact with numerous species
- Prevented the Borg from assimilating Earth (2366-2367)
- Mediated countless diplomatic disputes
- Explored unknown regions of space
- Defended the Federation against multiple threats

### Borg Assimilation

One of the most traumatic experiences of Picard's career occurred in 2366 when he was captured and assimilated by the Borg, becoming "Locutus of Borg." Although he was eventually rescued, the psychological scars from this experience would remain with him for years.

### USS Enterprise-E

After the Enterprise-D was destroyed in 2371, Picard was given command of the Sovereign-class USS Enterprise-E in 2372. He continued to serve with distinction, participating in critical missions including:

- Preventing another Borg invasion of Earth
- First contact with the Ba'ku
- Confronting his clone, Shinzon of Remus

## Command Philosophy

Picard is known for his thoughtful, diplomatic approach to command. He believes strongly in:

- The Prime Directive and non-interference
- Peaceful resolution of conflicts whenever possible
- The value of culture, art, and archaeology
- The potential for growth and redemption in all beings

## Personal Interests

- **Archaeology**: Picard has a deep passion for archaeology, particularly the ancient Taguan and Iconian cultures
- **Shakespeare**: An avid reader and performer of Shakespearean works
- **Dixon Hill**: Enjoys holodeck programs featuring the 1940s detective Dixon Hill
- **Earl Grey Tea**: His beverage of choice, always "hot"
- **Fencing**: Maintains proficiency as a fencer

## Legacy

Jean-Luc Picard's influence on Starfleet and the Federation cannot be overstated. His commitment to peaceful exploration, his respect for diverse cultures, and his unwavering moral compass have inspired generations of Starfleet officers.
