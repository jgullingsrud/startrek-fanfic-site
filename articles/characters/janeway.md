# Captain Kathryn Janeway

**Captain Kathryn Janeway** commanded the USS Voyager during its historic seven-year journey home from the Delta Quadrant. As one of Starfleet's most accomplished captains and the first female captain to headline a Star Trek series, Janeway combined scientific brilliance, tactical genius, and unwavering moral courage to bring her crew home against impossible odds.

## Background

- **Full Name**: Kathryn Janeway
- **Born**: May 20, 2335, Bloomington, Indiana, Earth
- **Rank**: Vice Admiral (by 2379)
- **Notable Command**: USS Voyager (2371-2378)
- **Education**: Starfleet Academy, Advanced Tactical Training
- **Family**: Father (Vice Admiral), Mother (civilian), Sister Phoebe (artist)

## Command of USS Voyager

### The Caretaker Incident (2371)

Voyager's first mission became a seven-year odyssey:

**Initial Mission**:
- Hunt for Maquis ship in Badlands
- First mission as Voyager's captain
- Crew of 150 personnel
- Expected to be brief assignment

**Transported to Delta Quadrant**:
- Pulled 70,000 light years from home
- By alien entity called the Caretaker
- Faced with impossible choice
- Could return home by sacrificing Ocampa species

**The Decision**:
- Destroyed Caretaker's array
- Saved Ocampa from Kazon
- Stranded crew in Delta Quadrant
- Chose principles over convenience
- Would take 70+ years to get home at maximum warp

**Merging Crews**:
- Combined Starfleet and Maquis crews
- Integrated former enemies
- Maintained Starfleet protocols
- United crew under single purpose: getting home

### The Long Journey Home (2371-2378)

**Seven-Year Mission**:
- Traveled 70,000 light years
- Made it home in seven years
- Faced countless dangers
- Lost crew members but brought most home
- Explored Delta Quadrant

## Final Assessment

Captain Kathryn Janeway accomplished the impossible: she brought her crew home from the other side of the galaxy, maintained Starfleet ideals in the face of impossible odds, and never compromised her values despite countless opportunities to take easier paths.

She proved that a captain's strength comes not from power but from principle, not from force but from compassion, and not from following orders but from doing what's right. She showed that determination, intelligence, and moral courage can overcome any obstacle.

Janeway didn't just get her crew home—she kept them together as a family, maintained their humanity in the face of dehumanizing challenges, and showed that even stranded in the Delta Quadrant, they could represent the best of Starfleet.
